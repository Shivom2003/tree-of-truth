# NAVIGATION_PATHS.md — Tree of Truth

> How users move through the tree. Not a sitemap — a map of meaning.
> Every path should feel like discovery, not navigation.

*Document version: 1.0 — Phase 0*

---

## Principles

1. **No dead ends.** Every section ends with a natural next move — deeper into the same tradition, or across to the convergence point.
2. **The tree is bidirectional.** A scientist can reach Nisargadatta. A Vedantin can reach Donald Hoffman. The paths work in both directions.
3. **The convergence is earned, not announced.** Users should arrive at the Roots ↔ Branches convergence through their own journey, not be told about it on a landing page.
4. **Every leaf points back to the tree.** A thinker profile is never a terminal node — it always connects to at least one section and at least one other thinker.
5. **Fruit is always one step away.** No matter where a user is, a practice, reading path, or journaling prompt should be reachable within one click.

---

## Entry Points

Users can enter the site through six distinct doors:

| Entry point | Who typically enters here | First content encountered |
|-------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **The Trunk** | Direct arrivals; people who encounter the root question | Root question + navigation prompt |
| **A Branch** | Science-first readers; people following a link from an article | A specific Branch section (B1–B6) |
| **A Root** | Tradition-first readers; people searching a specific practice or teacher | A specific Root section (R1–R5) |
| **A Leaf** | Thinker-first readers; people searching a specific name | A specific thinker profile |
| **Fruit** | Practice-first readers; people searching for meditation or reading lists | Fruit section (F1–F5) |
| **Search** | Intent-driven; people with a specific question | Closest matching section or thinker |

## The Five Primary Paths

These are the named narrative journeys through the tree. Each has a user archetype, an entry point, a sequence, and a destination. The destination is always the same: the recognition that ancient inquiry and modern science are circling the same thing.

---

### Path 1 — The Seeker Path
*"I feel like something is missing. I don't know what I'm looking for, but I know I'm looking."*

**Archetype:** Someone in genuine existential search. Not academic. Not looking for information — looking for orientation.

**Entry:** The Trunk (root question)

**Sequence:**
1. **Trunk** — Root question lands. Two navigation prompts: *enter through the Roots* or *enter through the Branches*.
2. **Direct Inquiry (R3)** — The seeker is drawn to the method before the tradition. The deep sleep inquiry and the neti neti negation are immediately recognizable as pointing at something real.
3. **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** — The tradition behind the method. Ramana and Nisargadatta as the primary voices. The I Am as the last foothold.
4. **Ramana Maharshi leaf** or **Nisargadatta leaf** — The seeker follows the voice that resonates.
5. **Fruit (F1 — practices)** — Self-inquiry as practice. The seeker leaves with something to do, not just something to know.
6. *Optional extension:* Panpsychism & Idealism (B5) — the seeker discovers that modern philosophy has arrived at the same place.

**Bridge text from R3 → R1:**
> *"The method points toward a tradition. For two thousand years, this same inquiry was the heart of Advaita Vedanta — the non-dual understanding that the one doing the inquiring and the ground of all being are not two."*

**Bridge text from R1 → B5 (optional):**
> *"Centuries later, analytic philosophy followed its own rigorous methods to the same edge. The conclusion was almost identical — arrived at from entirely the opposite direction."*

---

### Path 2 — The Scientific Path
*"I'm interested in the neuroscience or philosophy of consciousness. I follow the evidence."*

**Archetype:** A scientist, philosopher, or scientifically-minded reader who is skeptical of spiritual claims but genuinely curious about consciousness.

**Entry:** Neuroscience (B1) or Philosophy of Mind (B2) — likely via search or an external link.

**Sequence:**
1. **Neuroscience (B1)** — The empirical project. What has been found. The binding problem and the explanatory gap as the honest limit of the science.
2. **Philosophy of Mind (B2)** — The hard problem. Chalmers' formulation. The recognition that this is not a problem of insufficient data.
3. **Panpsychism & Idealism (B5)** — What follows from taking the hard problem seriously. Goff, Kastrup, Hoffman. Modern philosophy's convergence on consciousness-first.
4. **Kastrup leaf** or **Goff leaf** — The thinker who makes the argument most precisely.
5. *Convergence bridge to Roots:* **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** — The reader discovers that what Kastrup calls analytical idealism, Shankara called Advaita two thousand years ago.
6. **Direct Inquiry (R3)** — The scientific reader, now at the philosophical edge, encounters the method. Not belief — direct investigation.

**Bridge text from B2 → B5:**
> *"The hard problem does not resolve within materialism. Several of the philosophers who formulated it most precisely — Chalmers, Goff, Kastrup — have moved toward positions that reverse the usual assumption: not that consciousness emerges from matter, but that matter appears within consciousness."*

**Bridge text from B5 → R1:**
> *"This is not a new conclusion. What analytic philosophy is arriving at now, Advaita Vedanta articulated in systematic form in the 8th century. The language is different. The territory is the same."*

**Bridge text from R1 → R3:**
> *"The Vedantic tradition did not leave this as a philosophical claim. It developed a direct method of investigation — not argument, but inquiry. The same question the hard problem asks from the outside, self-inquiry asks from the inside."*

---

### Path 3 — The Tradition Path
*"I come from a contemplative background — Vedanta, Buddhism, or meditation practice. I want to understand how modern science relates to what I know from the inside."*

**Archetype:** A meditator, practitioner, or someone already familiar with one of the root traditions. Looking for the intellectual scaffolding that connects their practice to the broader conversation.

**Entry:** A Root section (R1, R2, or R3) — likely via search for a teacher or tradition.

**Sequence (from Vedanta entry):**
1. **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** — Home territory. But the section ends with a bridge: *"This same recognition is being reached, from a different direction, in contemporary philosophy and physics."*
2. **Panpsychism & Idealism (B5)** — Kastrup's explicit mapping of analytical idealism to Advaita. The practitioner sees their understanding reflected in analytic language.
3. **Philosophy of Mind (B2)** — The hard problem as the academic formulation of what the practitioner knows experientially as the witness.
4. **Quantum Mind (B4)** — Faggin and Hoffman. Physics arriving at consciousness-first.
5. **Faggin leaf** or **Hoffman leaf** — The practitioner's worldview meeting the physicist's.

**Sequence (from Buddhist entry):**
1. **Buddhist Inquiry (R2)** — Anatta, śūnyatā. The tradition.
2. **Phenomenology (R4)** — Metzinger's no-self through neuroscience and phenomenology. The practitioner sees anatta in the language of the self-model.
3. **Cognitive Science (B3)** — Varela and Thompson's enactivism emerging directly from Buddhist-phenomenological dialogue.
4. **Direct Inquiry (R3)** — The methodological bridge between traditions.

**Bridge text from R1 → B5:**
> *"What the Vedantic tradition arrived at through direct inquiry, contemporary Western philosophy is reaching through a different route. The convergence is not superficial — it is structural."*

**Bridge text from R2 → R4:**
> *"The Buddhist no-self teaching has a precise counterpart in 20th-century Western philosophy. Phenomenology and cognitive science arrived at a structurally identical conclusion — through entirely different methods."*

---

### Path 4 — The Thinker Path
*"I just read Kastrup / Chalmers / Ramana / Hoffman. I want to understand where they sit in the larger picture."*

**Archetype:** A reader who has encountered one thinker and wants context — who influenced them, who they influence, where their work connects to the broader inquiry.

**Entry:** A specific Leaf (thinker profile).

**The leaf profile always provides:**
- Which section(s) this thinker belongs to (with link)
- Which other thinkers they connect to (with links)
- A "where they sit on the spectrum" indicator

**Example: Entering via Chalmers leaf:**
1. **Chalmers leaf** — Hard problem formulation. Spectrum indicator: Deep Branches.
2. **Philosophy of Mind (B2)** — The section he belongs to. The broader landscape.
3. **Panpsychism & Idealism (B5)** — Where Chalmers has moved. Goff and Kastrup as the next step.
4. **Kastrup leaf** → **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** — Kastrup's explicit parallel.

**Example: Entering via Ramana leaf:**
1. **Ramana Maharshi leaf** — Self-inquiry, atma vichara. Spectrum indicator: Deep Roots.
2. **Direct Inquiry (R3)** — The section his method defines.
3. **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** — The tradition his method belongs to.
4. *Bridge to Branches:* **Panpsychism & Idealism (B5)** — Modern philosophy arriving at the same conclusion.
5. **Hoffman leaf** or **Kastrup leaf** — Thinkers at the convergence point, coming from the opposite direction.

**The specific journey from the project doc — Ramana → Donald Hoffman:**
> Ramana leaf → Direct Inquiry (R3) → cross-link: *"The same inquiry from the other side"* → Panpsychism (B5) → bridge text: *"Hoffman's Conscious Agents Theory arrives at what Ramana pointed at — through evolutionary game theory"* → Hoffman leaf.

---

### Path 5 — The Practice Path
*"I want to meditate, or I want a reading list. I'm not interested in theory right now."*

**Archetype:** A practitioner-first reader. Wants to do something, not just understand something.

**Entry:** Fruit section (F1 practices or F2 reading paths).

**Sequence:**
1. **Fruit (F1 — Meditation Practices)** — Self-inquiry, vipassana, dzogchen, non-dual. Each practice links to its tradition.
2. **Direct Inquiry (R3)** — From the practice of self-inquiry, naturally, to the section that contextualizes it.
3. **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** or **Buddhist Inquiry (R2)** — The tradition behind the practice.
4. **Spira leaf** or **Sarvapriyananda leaf** — A specific teacher to follow.

**Sequence (reading path entry):**
1. **Fruit (F2 — Reading Paths)** — "I come from science" path starts with Seth's *Being You*.
2. Follows the curated sequence: Seth → Kastrup → Chalmers → Panpsychism (B5) → Advaita (R1).

### "How does a user who enters via 'What is consciousness?' reach Nisargadatta?"

*"What is consciousness?"* is a search query or a question typed into the site's search.

**Route A — via the Branches:**
1. Search: "What is consciousness?" → **Philosophy of Mind (B2)** (the hard problem is the academic formulation of this question)
2. B2 ends with the explanatory gap → bridge to **Panpsychism & Idealism (B5)**
3. B5 includes Kastrup's explicit parallel to Vedanta → bridge to **Advaita Vedanta (R1)**
4. R1 features Nisargadatta as one of its primary voices → **Nisargadatta leaf**
5. The leaf's key quote: *"What are you before you think you are something?"* — the question has arrived at its most direct formulation.

**Route B — via the Trunk:**
1. Search: "What is consciousness?" → **Trunk** (root question page)
2. Trunk's navigation prompt: *"Enter through the Roots — the oldest and deepest answers"*
3. → **Direct Inquiry (R3)** — the most immediate methodological response to the question
4. R3 prominently features Nisargadatta's "I Am" → **Nisargadatta leaf**

---

### "How does a user who enters via 'Ramana Maharshi' reach Donald Hoffman?"

**Route:**
1. Search: "Ramana Maharshi" → **Ramana Maharshi leaf**
2. Leaf → **Direct Inquiry (R3)** (his method) and **Advaita Vedanta (R1)** (his tradition)
3. R1 ends with the convergence bridge: *"This is where ancient inquiry and modern science meet. Contemporary philosophy has arrived at a structurally identical conclusion — through entirely different means."* → **Panpsychism & Idealism (B5)**
4. B5 features Hoffman's Conscious Agents Theory alongside Kastrup and Goff
5. Bridge text in B5: *"Hoffman's mathematical framework for conscious agents is, in philosophical terms, Ramana's pointing rendered in the language of evolutionary game theory. The pointer is different. The direction is the same."* → **Donald Hoffman leaf**

## Convergence Points

These are the nodes where multiple paths meet. They are the most important pages on the site — where the argument of the whole project becomes visible.

### Primary convergence: R1 Advaita Vedanta ↔ B5 Panpsychism & Idealism

This is the central argument of the tree: that the most rigorous Western analytical philosophy of consciousness and the most rigorous ancient non-dual tradition are pointing at the same thing. Every path eventually touches this convergence.

**Design implication:** The cross-link between R1 and B5 should be the most prominent cross-link on the site. When a user is in R1 and follows the bridge to B5, or vice versa, it should feel like the moment the whole picture clicks into place.

### Secondary convergence: R3 Direct Inquiry

Every Root section connects to R3. Every Branch section that reaches its philosophical limit also connects to R3. Direct Inquiry is the methodological center of the tree — the place where all the arguments become a practice.

### Tertiary convergence: The Trunk

The Trunk is not where users arrive to learn — it is where users arrive after learning, to recognize what they have been circling. Some users will visit the Trunk first (as an entry point) and return to it later with different eyes.

## Mobile Navigation

On mobile, the visual tree is simplified. The full cross-link system is preserved, but accessed through a flat menu rather than the tree visualization.

**Mobile menu structure:**
- Roots → [R1] [R2] [R3] [R4] [R5]
- Branches → [B1] [B2] [B3] [B4] [B5] [B6]
- Thinkers (Leaves)
- Practices (Fruit)
- Search

**Mobile cross-links:** Each section page retains the same bridge texts and cross-links as desktop, presented as inline callout cards rather than animated tree connections.

## Search Behavior

Search is the highest-intent entry point — a user who searches knows what they are looking for. Search results should surface:

1. **Section pages** — if the query matches a topic (e.g., "hard problem", "self-inquiry", "neural correlates")
2. **Thinker profiles** — if the query matches a name (e.g., "Ramana Maharshi", "Kastrup", "Chalmers")
3. **Fruit content** — if the query is practice-oriented (e.g., "how to meditate", "self-inquiry practice", "reading list")
4. **The Trunk** — if the query is the root question or close to it (e.g., "what is consciousness", "what am I", "who is aware")

**Special case:** Queries that are themselves inquiry-like ("who am I", "what remains", "what is the self") should surface the Trunk page and Direct Inquiry (R3) as the primary results, not a keyword match. These are not search queries — they are the question.
